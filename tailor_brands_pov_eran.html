01
Google Cloud Israel · Hummus API

Creating Understanding

The market knew Google. It did not fully understand why Google Cloud mattered.

The challenge was not technology. It was perception.

Eran identified the gap and initiated a brand-led effort with Google Cloud leadership. The goal was not to explain cloud. It was to make Google Cloud easier to understand, remember, and talk about.

That became The Hummus API — a cultural entry point into a technical category.

Hundreds of thousands of YouTube views.
Mainstream and technology media coverage.
Industry-wide conversation.

Founder POV: Growth often starts by helping the market understand the value already there.

02
Google Cloud · Data Festival

Driving Adoption

For years, Google Cloud tried to win one of the largest freelancer marketplaces in the world.

The company was already operating on AWS. Another migration pitch would not move them.

The gap was not capability. It was turning capability into competitive advantage.

Through deep discovery, Eran identified the real opportunity: 50+ developers needed tools, skills, and confidence to move two steps ahead of the competition.

So instead of another deck, he built the Data Festival: a two-day experience with live AI demos, hands-on workshops, specialists, executives, developers, music, food, and energy.

12 AI initiatives launched.
Seven-figure cloud consumption followed.
The program became an EMEA blueprint.

Founder POV: People rarely change because they understand the plan. They change because they believe in the outcome.

03
Google Cloud · Multi-Million Dollar Partnership

Building Alignment

A global retail unicorn had built a category-defining AI platform reshaping retail and CPG.

At first, the conversation was narrow: infrastructure, pricing, vendor selection.

Eran saw a bigger opportunity. Why partner with Google Cloud instead of simply buying infrastructure?

He started with discovery: business objectives, growth strategy, product vision, competitive landscape, and long-term ambition.

The conversation moved from vendor evaluation to shared growth.

That became a custom global GTM motion: product collaboration, strategic customer access, shared pipeline, co-marketing, and mutual growth.

A multi-million dollar strategic partnership followed.

Founder POV: The strongest partnerships do not start with a pitch. They start with understanding.
